I am a quantitative biologist investigating how coordinated group phenomena emerge from the mechanics, signaling, and decision-making of individual bacterial cells. My approach combines mathematical modeling, microscopy, and microfluidics.

Research

Postdoc — Academia Sinica, Taiwan (2024–present)

Membrane potential dynamics in Bacillus subtilis biofilms

Biophysics Modeling Microfluidics Microscopy

I study how membrane potential is regulated in Bacillus subtilis, from individual cells to biofilm communities. To tackle this, I combine several complementary approaches:

  • Mathematical modeling — developing and analyzing equations to capture the biophysics of membrane potential regulation
  • Microfluidics — designing and fabricating custom microfluidic chips to constrain biofilm growth in two dimensions
  • Multiscale microscopy — imaging biofilms at both macroscopic and single-cell resolution to connect collective patterns to individual cell behavior
CNRS Postdoc — Laboratoire de Chimie Bacterienne, Marseille (2023–2024)

Characterizing collective organization in Myxococcus xanthus through topological defect analysis

Topological Defects Pattern Formation Image Analysis

I studied topological defects in the collective patterns of Myxococcus xanthus mutants to characterize organizational phenotypes. By quantifying defect distributions, I was able to discriminate between mutants with different organizational capacities.

PhD — Laboratoire de Chimie Bacterienne, Marseille (2020–2023)

Multi-scale analysis of multicellular transitions in the predatory bacterium Myxococcus xanthus

Collective Behavior Pattern Formation Modeling

During my PhD at the Laboratoire de Chimie Bacterienne (Institut de Microbiologie de la Mediterranee, Marseille), I investigated how Myxococcus xanthus, a soil-dwelling social bacterium, transitions between two collective movement patterns — rippling and swarming — at the boundary of prey colonies.

I elucidated how these two patterns can coexist with a sharp transition between them, and how single cells coordinate their responses to generate these distinct collective behaviors. This work combined experimental observations with mathematical modeling.
